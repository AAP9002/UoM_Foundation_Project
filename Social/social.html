Overview

An important part to a functional university living is the social component. Whether you are already a resident of Manchester or a prospective mancunian, knowing where to go, when to go and finding people to go with will be all part of the manchester experience that people cherish forever.

Starting a new course, attending lectures, seminars and going to workshops with other students will evetually result in having to be paired up and group work (for those who struggle socialising, this is a great way to make friends). But as much as one can avoid having to talk to people, and as scary as it may seem, it's going to have to be done so may as well kill two birds with one stone and make them friends!

Meeting People

As with anything, making friends in your university life is going to require effort. Successful friendships start with those who put themselves out there. Remember that most other student will just as nervous as making those lifelong bonds thta people cherish forever.

Despite all efforts however, there's no guaranteed formula that everyone that is met with a smile will be those who will be an instant click but there's no harm in trying. Quite possibly, those in the same course will have a lot more in common than previosuly notioned.

Making friends isn't easy. Not without some kind of effort. Rememebr to tag along with people who might be going out to coffee, talk to people in between lectures, don't be afraid to go out rather than staying in your room. Have those night outs and enjoy!

Societies

Struggling to fit in with anyone around campus? It would be worth to have a browse of the list societies avaiable. If nothing picks your fancy, then absoluteley start your own and promote it for others like-minded to join.

Societies are another source of opportuinity for those looking to create these lifelong bonds people keep talking about when reminising about universities. Whether you're a dancer, master chess player, love skiing, or just want to try something new that you haven't had the chance to, societies provide ample possiblilites for this. Meet like-minded people who wish to disucss a specific subject matter, enjoy an activity, or play a sport. With over 100 different societies, there'll certainly be something here for those looking

'Socials' set up by societes are also another great way to enjoy the company of other students. Most socieities organise events to allow their memebers to meet and greet. Go out, have fun with other members of socities.

Going Out

Exploring Manchester is a great way to discover its wonders. Now that the meeting people part is over, the great north wishes to be explored. With these list of options, where to go is now a question of the past.

Day Outings

Shopping in the City Centre

Shopping in Manchester is a great way to treat yourself. From Manchester Arndale to the infamous Trafford Centre, Manchester has it all. High street destinations such as Market Street and Exchange Square have shops all waiting to be visitied. A little bit of glamour never hurt anyone, by visiting the Jewellery qurater, dwelve into the delicacies Manchester has to offer!

Night Outs

42nd Street/The Venue

Manchester thrives in the clubbing scene, and 42s doesn't shy away from the reputation. With great indie anthems that everyone remebers from childhood, have sweat dripping off your head with a £1 vodka mixer in your hand is going to be an experience you'll never forget. These places are also great for getting off with someone so enjoy!

Image taken from The Mancunian

Hidden

From your first few weeks residing in the great north, you're going to hear about Hidden. Just on the outskirts of the city centre, in a place called Cheetham Hill, Hidden is one of the city's best and most progressive clubs. Offering nights every day of the week that pretty much satsifies everyone.

Places to Eat

Food and Manchester go hand-in-hand. A hotspot for all food and drink, yes we're a hungry bunch. From luxury steaks to affordable eats, we've narrowed down to three options that will be within budget for the average student.

Archies 115 Oxford Rd, Manchester M1 7DU

Manchester, the home of Archies. With curly fries from £2, Archie tots for £3.49 and chicken tenders £4.99, you'll be able to have burgers, fries, wraps and all of it for under a tenner!

Brewdog Outpost Manchester Business School, Oxford Road Manchester M13 9GH

Not every place has a Wings Wednesday all-you-can-eat wing buffet with plenty of veggie/vegan options on the menu that offer as a two-for-one on a Monday, but this isn't just every place. The orginal craft beer bros. presents Brewdog!

Milk & Honey St Peter s House, Oxford Road, Manchester M13 9GH

Right in the middle of our beloved UoM campus, Milk & Honey is a cafe with a difference. Veg soup for £3.75 from their plant-based menu, you can nourish the soul and mind with yoga and pilates here in this wellness cafe.
