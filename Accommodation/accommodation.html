Overview

The majority of students coming to university will be moving away from home for the first time which, while exciting, can also be a daunting experience. There are a variety of accommodation options to suit every individual's needs and wants.

Rooms can be catered or self catered, ensuite or shared bathrooms and come with different sized beds, desks and more. These rooms can be either booked through university accommodation (halls) or private accommodation.

Applications are simple and there's plenty of options within walking distance from the campus with convenient transport. Most accommodations offer bills included, utilities on site and a variety of rooms to suit your needs and budget. These are offered in 43 or 50 week contracts, spanning your academic year.

Halls

Our accommodation is set across three main areas, all within easy reach of the campus, the city and amenities. Each area offers something different but all offer a great place to live and study.

Starting university is an exciting time and living in university halls is a great way to meet other students, build friendships, and live with people from a variety of different backgrounds. Manchester halls of residence aren't just accommodation but their own communities, where many Manchester students make friends for life.

There are plenty of exciting events and opportunities life in halls has to offer. Every hall offers an extensive range of facilities, from computer clusters and libraries, to sports and bars, meaning you'll never be short of things to do or ways to socialise and relax.

Private

Choosing private accommodation can offer more control over the location, preferences and in some cases the ability to choose the exact room. Offering the same services as university accommodation, it is an ideal alternate. You can rent studios, 2-8 bed flats and houses, requesting to share flats with friends. The only difference from halls is you may be placed with students who have completed their first year.

Transport

There are Trams, Busses, Cycle lanes and Taxis available across greater Manchester getting you from A to B cheap and quick. If you're staying in accommodation, you'll most likely be served by stagecoach buses and the TFGM bike lanes. The university offers schemes and runs the 147 bus for free with your student card.

Stagecoach Bus Service

Stagecoach and its fleet of 700 buses in greater Manchester is the biggest public transport company in the area. With an app to track your bus live, it is convenient and reliable 24/7. Many of the buses are electric hybrid, moving towards TFGM goal to be net zero.

What To Expect

Room Mates

Moving in, everyone is nervous to meet new people and get to know the university. The first people you will likely meet are your roommates. With freshers week at the beginning and all the events offered, you'll have lots of opportunity to get to know them and form friendships. If you are worried about personal space, only you have access to your locked room and everyone establishes separate personal storage in the communal spaces.

Utilities - Laundry

Circuit Laundry offer washing and drying in many accomodation on site. The average price for washing is Â£3.20 and for drying Â£1.80 depending on the settings, which is managed on a mobile app making it easy to operate. As a guide, washing clothes every two weeks will be enough however this will widely vary.

Parcels

Any parcels are delivered to reception, where you can collect them anytime (depending on the accommodation). It is safe and reliable making receiving packages convenient for you. There are also local royal mail centres, parcel collection lockers and drop off boxes.
