What is the Integrated Foundation Year like?

Student Experiences

Manchester is a city to be explored. Once you've met people, now it's time to explore the great north. Where to go is now a question of the past with this great list of options to go to!

Andrew

Before attending Manchester university, I studied A level Computer science, Chemistry and Physics and as of now, I’ve had a good time in the foundation year. Comparing the foundation year to my previous school, the style of learning and approach has been almost the same with the quality of information and presentation has been great, but in the foundation year It has been straight to the point with topics and lectures being rather fast paced to ensure students get all the knowledge needed for exams in a small period. Regardless of this, the addition of coursework and project work does help alleviate some stress from the exams as not all the marks are dependent on an exam. During the project too, you build upon your team work skills and develop your individual skills making you a more confident and open person in group projects and group reliant activities both in and out of the course. I recommend the course for as it helps build upon skills you have and learn new skills required for [said] topic you would like to progress onto.

Alan

I studied A-Level Computer Science, Mathematics and Economics at College and have enjoyed the foundation year so far. The blended learning approach to content, delivered on blackboard online and live in-person sessions, works really well and gives you time to catch up. Being more structured than college, it is easier to plan and organise work, however you need to be disciplined to stay up to date. I did struggle with the science units, not having done them in A-level studies, but multiple coursework tests reduces the weight of exams which significantly helps. Group work was a skill I lacked, where this course addresses the importance of it. The skills I am learning will certainly help my further studies and I highly recommend this foundation year for Computer Science.

Zak

Coming out of A level college, I was disappointed that my grades weren’t as high as I’d hoped. If your situation sounds any bit similar to mine, then I would definitely recommend the Foundation Year course as an option for your degree course. Not only does it offer a “second chance” to get onto some of the UK’s most highly rated courses, you’ll also be armed with an excellent introduction to university life. This course will challenge you in new ways, recontextualising what you may have already seen into both the academic discipline and the university structure. I’ve personally found the foundation year to be challenging in all the right ways, and have been instrumental in developing both my individual and group skills. All the while I’ve found this course to be engaging and interesting, so I am very excited to continue my studies next year.

Maaz

The Integrated Foundation Year is very much a great opportunity for those who would like to have a place at University of Manchester despite not having the required grades. The grade requirements for Computer Science for example is A*A*A*, which not every student is capable of achieving no matter how talented an individual may be. So this course is one step in the door which will allow you to enter your chosen course. But it is also so much more than that. I think it’s a course designed to ensure you have every opportunity of passing as well as giving students a taste of the university life other students don’t really have the chance of having. I wouldn’t say it is at all a waste of a year as some may deem but an opportunity to understand university life and experience it fully.
